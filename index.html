SVCODEC:

A STREAMING VARIABLE NEURAL SPEECH CODEC


Huaifeng Zhang 1, Wenbin Jiang2, Peifei Wu1, Yuan An1, Zhen Wangn1 Tianpei Liu2, Hao Zhang1*,

1Ocean University of China 2Hangzhou Dianzi University

Abstract. This paper presents SVCODEC, a streaming neural speech codec with variable bit rate, which enables variable compression at bit rate from 0.5kbps to 4kbps. We integrate the encoder, decoder and residual vector quantizer into the generator, and then pair it with two sets of state-of-the-art discriminators to form a classic GAN network structure. After comparative testing with the same dataset, under the condition of low delay and low bitrate, SCVODEC's output speech has an absolute advantage in ViSQOL(3.45) PESQ(2.78) DNSMOS(3.30) and STOI(0.77) over traditional classic speech codec and existing neural speech codec. These indicators cover speech intelligibility, speech quality, speech distortion. The higher the score, the clearer the speech and the closer it is to the original speech. In other words, in very short encoding and decoding times, SvCodec can output very high-quality speech while also achieving variable compression ratios. So SvCodec just fills the research gap in marine satellite communications which has been limited by narrow bandwidth, multiple service frequencies, and low latency.

Overview

SVCODE model architecture. Part I:The generator (a) of the GAN network which consists of encoder (a1), vector quantizer (a2) and decoder (a3) and used to generate synthesized speech (y) which will be input to the discriminator (b) of the GAN network. Part II: The efficient discriminator (b), consisting of a multi-period discriminator (b1) and a multi-scale discriminator (b2).

Ethics Statement

Since our model could synthesize speech with great speaker similarity, it may carry potential risks in misuse of the model, such as spoofing voice identification or impersonating a specific speaker. We conducted the experiments under the assumption that the user agree to be the target speaker in speech synthesis. To prevent misuse, it is crucial to develop a robust synthesized speech detection model and establish a system for individuals to report any suspected misuse.
